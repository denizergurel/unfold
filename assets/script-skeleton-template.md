# Narration Script Skeleton (Phases 2 & 4)

Use this to draft and then ruthlessly cut. Target runtime: **3–7 minutes**
(roughly 450–1050 spoken words). Lean longer when the career has many beats
worth showing, shorter when the story rests on a few pivotal moments. Expect
your first pass to blow past the target — that's normal. Cut sections, not
just words.

Two shapes (pick one in Phase 2 — see
`references/phase-2-conversational-draft.md`):

- **Thematic** — 3–5 pivotal moments, ordered for impact. Use the thematic
  skeleton below.
- **Chronological arc** — walk the career in order, one slide per meaningful
  role or product. 12–20 slides. Use the chronological scaffold below.

## Chronological arc scaffold (12–20 slides)

Walk the career in order. One slide per meaningful role, product, or moment —
each with a real screenshot or artifact. The Phase 1 motif recurs as the
through-line.

- **Slide 1 — Hook + identity.** Current role and the motif in one line
  ("I build at the edge of what's possible.").
- **Slide 2 — Where you are now.** Brief frame of present work; sets context.
- **Slide 3 — Origin.** School / first job. The earliest sign of the motif.
- **Slides 4 → N–2 — One beat at a time, in order.** Each role or product
  gets its own slide. Personal asides woven in (a memorable decision, an
  off-brand pick) keep the arc human, not a CV read aloud.
- **Slide N–1 — What it all built up to.** The motif made explicit, briefly.
- **Slide N — Close.** Lands on fit for the target audience from Phase 1, or
  on what you want to keep building.
